This page provides a demonstration of Hepadnaviridae-GLUE being used to (i) organise the fossil record and; (ii) expand the fossil record to accomodate new data.

Organising the fossil record

The presence of ancient orthologous eHBV insertions in certain vertebrate clades (e.g. Neoaves, Serpentes, Testudines) quickly became apparent once the genome sequences of multiple species were available for analysis - because these sequences were incorporated into the germline prior to the radiation of major host lineages, they are present at the same genomic locus in all species within those lineages. The Hepadaviridae-GLUE resource forms an integral part of our framework for recovering the hepadnavirus fossil record. Firstly, it provides a stable source of ‘probe’ and ‘reference’ sequences for database-integrated genome screening (DIGS) [19].
